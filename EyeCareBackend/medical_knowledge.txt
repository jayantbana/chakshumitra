DISEASE: MYOPIA (Nearsightedness)
Symptoms: Blurry distance vision, squinting, headaches.
Causes: Eyeball is too long or cornea is too curved.
Treatment: Concave lenses (glasses/contacts), LASIK surgery.
Screening: Distance Acuity test showing worse than 20/40 vision.

DISEASE: PRESBYOPIA
Symptoms: Difficulty reading small print, holding phones further away.
Causes: Aging leads to hardening of the eye's lens.
Treatment: Reading glasses, bifocals, progressive lenses.
Screening: Near Vision test with N-Score worse than N6.

DISEASE: GLAUCOMA
Symptoms: Patchy blind spots in side (peripheral) vision. Advanced stages show tunnel vision.
Warning: "Silent Thief of Sight" - often no early symptoms.
Risk Factors: Age over 60, family history, high eye pressure.
Treatment: Prescription eye drops, laser treatment, surgery.
Emergency: Sudden severe eye pain with nausea requires immediate ER visit.
